print("\n\nQuantum Algorithmic Primitives:")

def quantum_fourier_transform_circuit(num_qubits=4):
    """QFT as algorithmic primitive."""
    circuit = Circuit(num_qubits, name="QFT")
    
    for i in range(num_qubits):
        circuit.h(i)
        for j in range(i + 1, num_qubits):
            circuit.cnot(i, j)
            circuit.rz(j, math.pi / (2 ** (j - i)))
    
    return circuit

circuit = quantum_fourier_transform_circuit(num_qubits=4)
print(f"QFT circuit depth: {circuit.depth}")

## Key Concepts

### Query Complexity
- Minimum queries needed to solve problem
- Quantum vs classical lower bounds
- Provable separations

### Algorithmic Primitives
- **QFT**: Foundation for many algorithms
- **Phase estimation**: Energy eigenvalues
- **Amplitude amplification**: Grover-type speedups

### Quantum Walks
- Discrete or continuous time
- Graph-based algorithms
- Potential speedups

### Applications
- **Optimization**: QAOA, annealing
- **Simulation**: Chemistry, physics
- **Machine learning**: QSVM, QPCA
- **Cryptography**: Shor's algorithm
